Basic Electrical Practice (बुनियादी विद्युत अभ्यास)

👨‍🏫 Classroom Note: Dosto, Basic Electrical Practice electrician trade ka sabse badhiya aur base chapter hai. Is note me water tank ke aasan udaharad se lekar Ohm's law aur AC/DC circuits ko aasan teacher style me samjhaya gaya hai. Ekदम dhyan se padhna!

Chapter 1 : Electricity Kya Hai?

Sabse Pehle Ek Simple Example (Water Tank Setup)

Socho tumhare ghar mein ek water tank hai aur tank se paani pipe ke through neeche aata hai. Agar isko electrical framework me samjhein to:

💧 Water = Electron
🚰 Pipe = Wire (Conductor)
💥 Pressure = Voltage
🌊 Water Flow = Current
🔌 Valve = Switch
🚧 Resistance = Pipe ka andar se patla ya blocked hona
Electricity Ki Definition
Electricity is the flow of electric charge (electrons) through a conductor.
Aasan Bhasha me: Electricity ek aisi energy hai jo electrons ke ek jagah se doosri jagah move karne par banti hai aur conductor ke through travel karti hai.

Matter aur Atom Ka Structure

Matter Kya Hota Hai?
Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space.
Duniya ki har cheez—chahe Chair, Table, Human body, Fan, Wire, ya Iron ho—sab Matter hai kyunki inki apni mass hoti hai aur ye jagah gherte hain.
Atom Aur Uske Parts

Har matter chhote particles se banta hai jise Atom kehte hain. Atom ke teen main parts hote hain:

➕ Proton: Positive charge (+1), jo Nucleus ke andar hota hai.
➖ Electron: Negative charge (-1), jo Nucleus ke bahar charo taraf ghoomta hai.
⭕ Neutron: Koi charge nahi hota (Neutral), ye bhi Nucleus me rehta hai.
Electron (-) ○ ○ ○ [ Nucleus ] Proton (+) Neutron (0) ○ ○ Electron (-)
Atom Core Structure Grid

Electron Kyu Move Karte Hain?

Har atom stable banna chahta hai. Jab kisi atom ke outer shell (baari kaksha) me loose ya extra electron hota hai to wo pressure paakar easily move kar jata hai. Ye moving electron hi electricity banata hai.

Materials Ke Types:
* Conductor (चालक): A material that allows electric current to pass through it easily. (Example: Copper, Silver, Gold, Aluminium, Iron). * Insulator (कुचालक): A material that does not allow electric current to pass. (Example: Plastic, Rubber, Glass, Wood, Air). * Semiconductor (अर्धचालक): A semiconductor has conductivity between conductor and insulator. (Example: Silicon, Germanium; jo Diode aur Transistor me use hota hai).

Electric Charge, Voltage aur Current

Electric Charge:

Charge do prakar ke hote hain: Positive (+) aur Negative (-). **Same charges (+ + / - -)** ek doosre ko door bhagate hain (Repel) aur **Opposite charges (+ -)** aapas me attract karte hain.

Voltage (V) - Electrical Pressure:
Voltage is the electrical pressure that pushes electrons through a conductor.
V = W / Q (Work / Charge)

Iski unit Volt hai, jise Alessandro Volta ke naam par rakha gaya tha.

Current (I) - Electron Ka Flow:
Current is the rate of flow of electric charge.
I = Q / t (Charge / Time)

Iski unit Ampere hai. **Direction Spec:** Actual electron flow Negative se Positive hota hai, lekin kitabon/exams me standard **Conventional Current (Positive to Negative)** ko hi mana jata hai.

Resistance aur Ohm's Law

Resistance (R):
Resistance is the opposition offered to the flow of electric current.

Jaise road par traffic jam gaadi ko rokta hai, waise hi resistance current ko rokta hai. Iski unit **Ohm ($\Omega$)** hoti hai.

Ohm's Law (ओम का नियम):

At constant temperature, current is directly proportional to Voltage and inversely proportional to Resistance.

V = I × R | I = V / R | R = V / I
Numerical Example: Agar Voltage = 12V hai aur Resistance = 6$\Omega$ hai, to Current kya hoga?
⚡ $I = V / R \implies 12 / 6 = \mathbf{2\text{ Ampere (A)}}$
[ V ] --------- I | R
Ohm's Law Calculation Matrix Triangle

Electrical Power aur Energy

Power (P):
Power is the rate of doing electrical work. (Electricity kitni fast consume ho rahi hai).
P = V × I | P = I²R | P = V² / R

Iski unit Watt hoti hai.
*Example:* Agar 220V supply par 5A current chal raha hai, to $Power = 220 \times 5 = \mathbf{1100\text{ Watt}}$.

Energy (E):

Power aur Time ke guna ko Energy bolte hain ($E = P \times t$). Iski commercial unit **kWh (Kilo-Watt-Hour)** hoti hai, jise hum aam bhasha me **1 Unit** bijli kehte hain.

AC Current vs DC Current (अंतर समझो)

Parameter AC (Alternating Current) DC (Direct Current)
Direction Iska direction aur value baar-baar badalta rehta hai. Iska direction aur value hamesha ek saman (straight) rehta hai.
Frequency India me standard 50 Hz hoti hai. Frequency bilkul 0 Hz hoti hai.
Source Alternator, Gharon ki supply (230V). Battery, Mobile cell, Solar panel.
Graph Line Wave form (Sine wave) 📈 Straight line horizontal ───

Series vs Parallel Circuit Combinations

Series Circuit (श्रेणी संयोजन):

Saare resistors ek hi line me end-to-end jude hote hain. Isme **Current same** rehta hai par **Voltage divide** ho jata hai.
Formula: $R_T = R_1 + R_2 + R_3$

Parallel Circuit (समांतर संयोजन):

Sabhi resistors ke siron ko alag alag aapas me parallel joda jata hai. Isme **Voltage same** rehta hai aur **Current divide** ho jata hai. Gharon ki wiring parallel me hoti hai.
Formula: $1/R_T = 1/R_1 + 1/R_2 + 1/R_3$

Circuit States, Protection & Safety Devices

* Open Circuit: Switch OFF hota hai, current zero rehta hai. * Closed Circuit: Switch ON hota hai, current smoothly flow karta hai. * Short Circuit: Jab Phase aur Neutral bina kisi load ke aapas me touch ho jayein. Resistance zero ho jata hai aur current bohot zyada high, jisse aag lagne ka khatra hota hai. * Fuse: Melts when excess current flows. Ye lead-tin alloy ka safety device hai. * MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker): Modern fuse version jo high current par trip ho jata hai aur ise dobara ON kiya ja sakta hai. * Earthing: Leakage current ko zameen me bhejkar insani body ko electric shock se bachana.

Chapter Summary (One-Line Revision)

⚡ Electricity = Electron ka continuous flow
⚛️ Atom Core = Proton (+) + Neutron (0) + Electron (-)
💥 Voltage = Pushing pressure unit
🌊 Current = Flow velocity rate
🚧 Resistance = Current path opposition
📐 Ohm's Law = Formula grid equation $V = IR$
🔄 AC Supply = Direction periodically changes (50 Hz)
🔋 DC Supply = Constant unidirectional movement (0 Hz)
🛡️ Fuse/MCB = Melt/Trip protective switch line
🟢 Earthing Node = Instant shock safety ground path

5. Master CBT Top 10 Exam Core Q&A Bank

Q1. Electro-motive Force (EMF) ki SI unit kya hoti hai?
Ans: Volt. Ye wahi pressure hai jo electron ko chalata hai.
Q2. Atom ka nucleus kin particles se milkar banta hai?
Ans: Protons aur Neutrons se. Electrons nucleus ke bahar chakkar lagate hain.
Q3. Conventional current flow ka direction actual electron movement se kaise alag hota hai?
Ans: Actual electron flow Negative se Positive hota hai, jabki Conventional current hamesha Positive se Negative chalta hai.
Q4. Ohm's Law electrical networks me kis component par lagoo nahi hota?
Ans: Semiconductor devices (jaise Diode, Transistors) aur non-linear loads par.
Q5. Bijli ke 1 Unit meter consumption ka technical matlab kya hota hai?
Ans: 1 kWh (Kilo-Watt-Hour) electrical energy ki matra.
Q6. Short circuit ki sthiti me current aur resistance ka maan kitna ho jata hai?
Ans: Resistance lagbhag Zero ($\Omega$) ho jata hai aur Current anant (Very High) ho jata hai.
Q7. India me domestic AC power supply ki voltage aur frequency standard kya hai?
Ans: 230V AC aur 50 Hz frequency curve.
Q8. Series circuit me agar ek bulb kharab ho jaye, to baaki bulbs par kya asar padega?
Ans: Baaki saare bulbs bhi band ho jayenge kyunki current ka single rasta break ho jata hai.
Q9. Fuse wire ko hamesha phase wire ke sath kis sequence me joda jata hai?
Ans: Series (श्रेणी) kram me joda jata hai taaki overload hote hi circuit break ho sake.
Q10. Humari body ko metallic body leakage shock se bachane ke liye kaun si safety process zaruri hai?
Ans: Equipment Earth Grounding yaani Earthing connection process.
