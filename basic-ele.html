1. Electricity Kya Hai? (Deep Deep Analysis)

Electricity koi single entity nahi hai balki physics me ye charges ki static ya dynamic property hoti hai. Jab kisi closed circuit path me Free Electrons ek systematic internal acceleration ke sath ek disha me flow karte hain, to us dynamic sequence ko hum Electric Current / Electricity kehte hain.

Atomic Depth Matrix:

Kisi bhi material ka sabse chhota operational part Atom hai. Nucleus ke charo taraf alag-alag orbits (K, L, M, N) hote hain. Valence Shell (Sabse bahari kaksha) me moujood electrons ko agar thodi si energy (ionization potential) mil jaye, to wo atom ko chhodkar poorie crystalline lattice me ghoomne lagte hain. Inhi ko hum Free Electrons kehte hain.

🧪 Material Reality: Copper ke paas $8.5 \times 10^{28}$ free electrons per cubic meter hote hain. Yahi wajah hai ki ye ek shandar conductor hai!

2. Voltage, Current aur Resistance Ka Vector Relationship

A. Voltage (Potential Difference - V):

Keval electrons ka hona kafi nahi hai, unhe dhakelne ke liye energy ki zarurat hoti hai. Kisi charge $Q$ ko ek point se doosre point tak le jaane me kiya gaya work done hi Voltage (Potential Difference) kehlata hai.

V = W / Q [Joules / Coulomb = Volt]
B. Current (I):

Rate of change of charge over time. Yaani ek second me cross-sectional area se kitne coulomb charge flow ho gaye.

I = Q / t = n × e / t [Ampere]

*CRITICAL SPEC: Actual drift velocity of electron bohot slow hoti hai ($\approx 1\text{ mm/s}$), par pure circuit me electromagnetic wave lightning speed ($3 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$) se travel karti hai, jisse instantaneous propagation hota hai.

C. Resistance (R):

Lattice ions ke sath electrons ke continuously hone wale collisions (takarav) hi resistance ka mukhya kaaran hain.

R = ρ × (L / A) [Ohm (Ω)]
📐 Parameters Matrix:
• ρ (Resistivity): Nature of material.
• L (Length): Length badhne par collisions badhenge (R ∝ L).
• A (Area): Area badhne par rasta chouda hoga (R ∝ 1/A).

3. Ohm's Law Aur Electrical Power Calculations

George Simon Ohm ke mutabik agar physical conditions (jaise temperature, pressure) constant rahein, to kisi conductor se behne wala current uske siran par lagaye gaye Potential Difference ke directly proportional hota hai.

V ∝ I ⇒ V = I × R
Electrical Power Matrix (P):

Kisi active element dwara consume ya deliver kiye jaane wale work done ki time rate ko power kehte hain.

P = V × I = I²R = V² / R [Joules/sec = Watt]
Energy Parameter (E):

Power jab time period tak active rehti hai to energy consume hoti hai. $E = P \times t$. Iska commercial parameter **1 kWh = $3.6 \times 10^6\text{ Joules}$** hota hai.

4. Direct Current (DC) vs Advanced Alternating Current (AC)

Dono current type me polarization aur behavior ka zameen-aasman ka antar hota hai:

Frequency Core Concept:

AC me wave jitne complete cycles (one positive half and one negative half) ek second me poore karti hai, use Frequency ($f$) kehte hain. Iska time period $T = 1/f$ hota hai. India me standard frequency 50 Hz hai, yaani current 1 second me 100 baar apni disha badalta hai.

Wahi dusri taraf, DC ek unidirectional steady line state hai jisme samay ke sath polarities nahi badalti, isliye DC ki frequency hamesha 0 Hz hoti hai.

5. Power Factor (पावर फैक्टर - Sabse Important Concepts)

AC circuits me inductive ya capacitive loads ke karan Voltage aur Current waveforms ke beech ek phase angle gap ban jata hai jise hum $\theta$ kehte hain. Is phase difference ke cosine angle ko hi Power Factor ($\cos\theta$) kaha jata hai.

Power Factor (PF) = Real Power (kW) / Apparent Power (kVA) = R / Z
Power Ke Teen Roop (The Triangle Grid):

1. True / Active Power ($P$): Jo actually work karti hai. Unit: **kW** ($P = VI\cos\theta$).
2. Reactive Power ($Q$): Jo magnetic field banane me lagti hai aur source me vaapas chali jaati hai. Unit: **kVAR** ($Q = VI\sin\theta$).
3. Apparent Power ($S$): Total combined power supply line capability. Unit: **kVA** ($S = VI = \sqrt{P^2 + Q^2}$).

Power Factor Ke Types:
• Unity (1.0): Purely Resistive load (jaise heater, incandescent bulb). Current aur voltage sath-sath chalte hain.
• Lagging (Pichhe): Inductive load (jaise Motors, Fan, Transformer). Current, voltage se pichhe reh jata hai.
• Leading (Aage): Capacitive load. Current, voltage se aage nikal jata hai.
