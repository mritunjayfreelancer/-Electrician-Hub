1. What is Electricity? (बिजली क्या है?)

English Definition
Electricity: Electricity is the flow of electric charges (electrons) through a solid or liquid conductor.
Hindi Translation
Electricity electrons ka safe flow hai jo conductor ke andar move karta hai. Agar simple language me kahein, to current ka continuous flow hi Electricity hai.

2. Types of Electricity (इलेक्ट्रिसिटी के प्रकार)

Electricity mukhyatah do prakar ki hoti hai:

Type Technical Description Real-World Examples
1. DC (Direct Current) Current sirf ek fixed Single Direction me flow karta hai. Iska magnitude change nahi hota. Battery, Mobile Battery, Solar Battery, UPS Battery, Car Battery.
2. AC (Alternating Current) Current apni direction aur value time ke saath continuously/periodically change karta rehta hai. Home Supply, Factory Supply, Office, Hospital, College Grid.

3. Deep Definition of AC (प्रत्यावर्ती धारा की परिभाषा)

AC Physics Mode
Alternating Current (AC): Alternating Current is an electric current whose magnitude and direction change periodically with respect to time.
सरल हिंदी परिभाषा
Alternating Current wo current hai jo apni direction aur numeric value dono ko time ke saath continuously badalta rehta hai.

4. Structural Difference: AC vs DC (अंतर)

Parameter AC (Alternating Current) DC (Direct Current)
Direction Changes continuously with time Stays in the same direction
Frequency Present (50 Hz / 60 Hz) Zero (0 Hz)
Transmission Easily transmitted to long distances safely Difficult & costly for long distances
Primary Use Used in Homes, Industries, Appliances Stored in Batteries, Electronic Circuits
Production Alternators / AC Generators Chemical Batteries / DC Generators

5. Why AC is Used? (एसी का उपयोग क्यों होता है?)

Teacher asks: "Baccho... Agar DC bhi electricity hai... To ghar me AC hi kyu aati hai?"

Core Industrial Reasons:

  • Easily transmitted over long distances safely.
  • Highly Cheap generation cost compared to DC.
  • Less Power Loss during heavy line transmission.
  • Voltage can be easily Increased/Decreased using a static Transformer device.
  • Lagbhag poori duniya ka power system isi efficiency ki wajah se AC par based hai.

6. AC Waveform Graph Parameters (साइन वेव ग्राफ)

Agar hum AC ko Graph me dekhein, to AC ki wave time frame ke saath upar (Positive Half) bhi jaati hai aur niche (Negative Half) bhi aati hai. Is wave graph shape ko Sinusoidal Wave (Sine Wave) kehte hain.

Important Alternating Terms (Interview Essentials):

1. Cycle (चक्र):
Ek complete positive aur ek complete negative wave loop ke combination ko One Cycle kehte hain.
2. Time Period (T - आवर्तकाल):
One complete cycle ko travel hone me jitna time lagta hai use Time Period kehte hain.
Unit: Second | Formula: T = 1 / f
3. Frequency (f - आवृत्ति):
1 second duration me total kitni cycles complete hui, use frequency kehte hain.
Unit: Hertz (Hz) | Formula: f = 1 / T
National Standards: India = 50 Hz | America = 60 Hz
(India me 50 Hz ka matlab hai ki current 1 second me 50 baar apni cycle complete karta hai!)

7. Electrical Fundamentals & Ohm's Law (ओम का नियम)

  • Voltage (V): Electrical pressure that pushes current through a conductor. Unit: **Volt**.
  • Current (I): The physical flow of electric charge (electrons). Unit: **Ampere**.
  • Resistance (R): Opposition offered by a material to the flow of current. Unit: **Ohm (Ω)**.

Ohm's Law Master Formula Matrix:

V = I × R  |  I = V / R  |  R = V / I

Practical Engineering Example:

Given Voltage (V) = 230V
Given Resistance (R) = 23Ω
Calculation: I = V / R → 230 ÷ 23 = 10 Ampere Current.

8. Types of AC Circuits (एसी सर्किट के प्रकार)

AC Supply line par kaam karne wale dynamic circuits mukhyatah nimnlikhit types ke hote hain:

Circuit Configuration Key Properties & Phase Behavior Industrial Practical Examples
1. Pure Resistive Circuit (R) Voltage aur Current absolute **Same Phase** me chalte hain. Phase Angle = 0°, Power Factor = **Unity (1)**, Loss is Minimum. Electric Heater, Electric Iron, Toaster, Incandescent Bulb.
2. Pure Inductive Circuit (L) Current, Voltage se **90° Late (Lag)** hota hai.
🎯 *Mnemonic Rule:* ELI (In Inductor, Voltage Leads Current). 		Motors, Transformers, Fluorescent Choke Coils, Inductive Fans.
3. Pure Capacitive Circuit (C) Current, Voltage se **90° Ahead (Lead)** hota hai.
🎯 *Mnemonic Rule:* ICE (In Capacitor, Current Leads Voltage). 		Capacitor Banks, Power Factor Correction Filters.
4. Combinations Systems Includes complex **R-L**, **R-C**, and **R-L-C Circuits** based on load conditions. Mixed Industrial Loads.

9. Single Phase vs Three Phase Supply Matrix

Technical Parameters Single Phase AC Supply Three Phase AC Supply
Conductor Layout Consists of 1 Phase + 1 Neutral Wire (2 Wires) Consists of 3 Phases + Optional Neutral (3/4 Wires)
Standard Voltage 230 Volt Index 415 Volt Index (Line Voltage)
Phase Separation No phase split parameters 3 Voltages having equal magnitude but **120° Phase Difference**
Power Capacity Low power configuration limits Ultra High Power efficiency capacity
Primary Field Application Home, Shop, Offices, TV, Fan, Fridge Heavy Factories, Heavy Motors, Compressors, Pumps

10. AC Power Formulas & Power Factor

Single Phase Power Formula: P = V × I × cosφ
Three Phase Power Formula: P = √3 × V × I × cosφ

What is Power Factor (cosφ)?

Power factor yeh batata hai ki infrastructure ki total supply power me se kitni power practical useful work me consume ho rahi hai. Iska control value index 0 se 1 ke beech hota hai. Ideal case me power factor hamesha **1 (Unity)** hona chahiye.

💬 Viva-Voce Technical Interview Preparation (Top 10 Questions)

Q1. What is AC?
Ans: AC (Alternating Current) is an electric current whose magnitude and direction change periodically with time.
Q2. What is the frequency of AC supply in India?
Ans: Standard frequency is 50 Hz.
Q3. What is the standard voltage rating of single-phase domestic lines?
Ans: 230 Volts.
Q4. What is the line voltage rating of a three-phase supply network?
Ans: 415 Volts.
Q5. What is the geometric phase displacement in a three-phase power system?
Ans: Exactly 120° electrical degrees phase difference between any two phase voltages.
Q6. Which specific circuit condition produces Unity Power Factor?
Ans: Pure Resistive Circuit (Load contains only resistance like heaters).
Q7. In a pure inductive circuit, which variable leads?
Ans: Voltage leads the current exactly by 90° angle parameter.
Q8. In a pure capacitive load circuit, which variable leads?
Ans: Current leads the applied voltage exactly by 90° angle window.
Q9. Why is a three-phase system strictly preferred for driving industrial motors?
Ans: Because three-phase lines deliver smooth constant torque, operate with higher efficiency, and lower overall line transmission losses.
Q10. State the conceptual definition of Ohm's Law.
Ans: Provided physical conditions like temperature remain constant, the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to voltage across its ends and inversely proportional to its resistance ($I = V/R$).
